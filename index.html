City Voice

City Voice displays the prevalence of citizen engagement throughout the city of Cambridge, MA. It uses publicly available information on written comments from individuals around the area to their city government in order to see where the most active and engaged residents tend to reside - and how this changes over the years.

We can see that throughout the past five years, much of the public discourse has been dominated by the south and north-west regions of the city, where the bulk of long-term residents live, with large "holes" near MIT and Harvard, where many of the more transient student populations live.

As the years go on, however, and the East Cambridge/Kendall Square areas became more developed, a much larger presence from the newly populated tech hubs came into the picture - especially as the existing residents suddenly had much to say about construction noise, under or overdevelopment, and/or resulting resident displacement.

This display helps to visualize how public discourse can indicate both entrenched long-term trends in the demographic characteristics of active citizens around the city as well as how neighborhood development can spur evolutionary change in these trends.

Designed and developed by Dana Bullister for ARTG6100 Information Design Studio 2: Dynamic Mapping and Models, Spring 2021, IDDV Program, Northeastern University
